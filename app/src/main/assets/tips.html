Top Tips for traveling in Germany

1) Make sure you learn some basic phrases before arriving

Germans are known for being direct, which means they won’t beat around the bush when speaking with others. The best way to avoid an embarrassing situation is by learning a few basic phrases and words before arriving.

2) Be aware of the cultural norms

Germans have a very different way of doing things, so it is important to be aware of the cultural norms before traveling. For example, in Germany it is considered very impolite to be late, so make sure you arrive at your meeting or dinner on time. Make sure you understand German punctuality and the importance of structure. Additionally, Germans are known for being honest and straightforward, so don’t be surprised if they tell you that your outfit looks silly or that your idea isn’t very good.

3) Try out the local food

German food is world-famous for a reason! From bratwurst and sauerkraut to apple strudel and Black Forest cake, there is something for everyone to enjoy. The good thing is that generally speaking is that you’ll find cheap food regardless of whether it’s luxury German food or German street food.

4) Take time to explore the countryside

Germany is a beautiful country with lots of stunning scenery to explore. From the rolling hills of the Bavarian Alps to the quaint villages in the Black Forest, there is something for everyone.

5) Visit a beer garden

Germans love their beer, and what better way to experience the German culture than by visiting a traditional beer garden?

6) Attend a festival

Germany is home to some of the world’s most famous festivals, such as Oktoberfest and the Christmas markets. There are many other festivals in Germany that are worth visiting. One of the most famous is the Carnival of Cologne, which takes place in February. The festival is a 5-day celebration that includes parades, parties, and a beauty contest. Another popular festival is the Berlin Beer Festival, which takes place every August. The festival features more than 300 different types of beer from around the world.

7) Ride the train

The German railway system is one of the best in the world, so be sure to take a ride on the train while you’re in the country. You’ll get to see some amazing scenery and you’ll be able to get around quickly and easily. You will also save money along the way since train transportation is very affordable within the country. Consider getting a rail pass to save even more and ride as much as you want for a single cost.

8) Visit a castle or castles

Germany is home to some of the most beautiful castles in the world, so be sure to visit at least once while you’re in the country. From the imposing Neuschwanstein Castle (the Disney castle was modeled after this) to the fairytale-like Hohensalzburg Castle, there is something for everyone. There are over 20,000 castles in Germany, so there is no shortage of options when it comes to choosing one to visit. Whether you’re looking for a fairytale-like castle or an imposing medieval fortress, you’ll be able to find it in Germany. Be sure to add at least one castle visit to your itinerary while you’re in the country.

10) Shop till you drop

Germany is home to some of the best shopping in the world and is a perennially underrated shopping destination. From high-end designer stores to traditional German markets, you’ll be able to find something for everyone. Be sure to do some shopping while you’re in Germany, you won’t regret it.
